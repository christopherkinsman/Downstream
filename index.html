Downstream Rules

For more information, read the official rules here.

GOAL

Two players will take turns adding cards to a river environment in an attempt to create the most scenic shore on their side of the river.

Cards can be used to extend the flow of water further downstream or to contribute to the land on either side, enriching the area.

Games are played with a varying set of objectives which specify what features players should look to include along their river banks and how they should be placed.

The player who can achieve these objectives most successfully on their side of the river, wins.

GAMEPLAY

STEP ONE: TAKE A CARD

Click the river card deck to pick a card from the top of the deck.

Each players turn is indicated by a blue border around their corresponding card.

STEP TWO: PLAY CARDS

Click the board to place your card at the end of the river.

GAME END

The game ends when all cards have been played.

When both players have no cards to play, the game ends and scoring commences.

Scores are calculated by counting the points earned by each player.
